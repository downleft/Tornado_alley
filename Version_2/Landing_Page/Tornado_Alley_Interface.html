Tornado Alley

An analysis of tornado frequency within the US since 1950 using data retrieved from Kaggle and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)

Introduction

Tornadoes are a destructive force for which Tornado Alley has been named. Data for these storms has been gathered by NOAA and is available dating back to 1950.

This project seeks to analyze the data from NOAA and Kaggle to answer the following questions:

1. Where have tornadoes occurred the most? Has this region moved?

2. Has the frequency of tornadoes changed?

3. Has the intensity of storms changed?

4. When do tornadoes occur the most?

State Analysis

Since 1950, Texas has experienced the greatest number of tornadoes in the United States. Florida experienced the third greatest number of tornadoes from 1950-89 and 1990-2009, but dropped outside of the top 10 from 2010-2023. Kansas and Oklahoma were both in the top 5 for tornado occurrence from 1950-1989, 1990-2009, and 2010-2023. From 1950-2009, Mississippi was ranked outside of the top 10. From 2010-2023, Mississippi had the third highest number of tornadoes.

By region, from 1950-1989, 6 of the top 10 states for tornadic activity were in the Midwest, and 4 of the top 10 were in the South. From 1990-2009, 6 of the top 10 states were in the Midwest, with 3 in the South, and Colorado as the lone Western state in the top 10. From 2010-2023, 5 of the top 10 states for tornadic activity were in the South, and 5 of the top 10 states for tornadic activity were in the Midwest.

National Trend

Nationally, the US has a positive trend in the number of recorded tornadoes per year, though this may be due in part to the advancement of technology. From 1990-2009, the US has seen a slightly negative trend in annual tornadic activity, with noticeable peaks in 2004, 2008, and 2011.
